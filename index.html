Phase 1

Reconnaissance – The Forensic Baseline

Before investigating a crime scene, you document the scene as found. We interview stakeholders across product, engineering, sales, support, and data — then gather every artifact that shaped the product.

Stakeholder Alignment

  • Product leadership: what would "fixed" look like in measurable terms?
  • Engineering: known technical debts affecting UX
  • Customer success: top 10 support ticket categories
  • Sales: objections prospects raise about the product
  • Data team: biggest drop-off points, retention curve shape

Deliverable: Stakeholder Perception Map – revealing internal misalignment that creates faultlines.

Artifact Collection & Metrics

  • PRDs, design files, user research reports
  • Support tickets (90+ days), app store reviews, NPS verbatims
  • Onboarding completion, feature adoption, A/B test results
  • Baseline metric matrix: activation, engagement, retention, support load, performance, sentiment

Deliverable: Quantitative Baseline Report – the product's vital signs before investigation.

Forensic lens: We read between the lines of user complaints. “Confusing” often means mental model mismatch, violated predictions, or missing feedback — not just bad UI.
Phase 2

Investigation – Dual‑Track Evaluation Engine

Expert heuristic judgment and algorithmic data analysis run simultaneously. We look for convergence (high confidence) and divergence (hidden anomalies).

Track A – Heuristic Evaluation

FoundationBusiness‑ImpactPsychological

Three-tier system beyond Nielsen’s 10:

  • Foundation: Extended Nielsen heuristics with forensic cost estimation
  • Business‑Impact: Trust architecture, cognitive load architecture, failure mode dignity, scale‑induced debt, adoption gravity, cross‑channel coherence
  • Psychological: Relevance realization, predictive processing alignment, loss aversion architecture, cognitive load distribution, agency & autonomy

Track B – Algorithmic & Data Forensics

  • Funnel & revenue forensics: where value leaks
  • Rage click & dead zone analysis
  • Session recording patterns (hesitation, re‑reading, tab switching)
  • Support ticket forensics: failure demand %, vocabulary of confusion, workaround index
  • Competitive benchmark mapping
  • Performance/technical UX impact (Core Web Vitals on critical flows)

Findings are mapped by source, severity, business impact, and confidence:

  • Confirmed (High): Heuristic + data alignment
  • Indicated (Medium): One track, plausible in other
  • Suspected (Low): Single source, absent/contradicted
  • Anomaly: Data signal without heuristic explanation → further investigation

Convergence = high confidence faultline. Divergence = hidden risk or measurement gap.

Phase 3

Diagnosis – Building the Fault Topology

Every finding is classified into five faultline types and plotted on a 2D Fault Map (journey stage × depth).

Surface – visible usability friction
Structural – IA/navigation problems
Trust – dark patterns, opaque behavior
Systemic – foundational design debt
Strategic – product–market misalignment

The Fault Map

Nodes are sized by business impact, colored by confidence. We instantly see where problems concentrate — are they surface-level onboarding hiccups, or systemic engagement fractures?

Topology reading: Trust faultlines across the entire journey signal a design philosophy problem, not just a UI bug.

Root Cause Analysis (5‑Whys)

For critical faults, we drill down to the organisational root cause:

  • User research gap
  • Stakeholder override
  • Technical constraint accepted without UX mitigation
  • Legacy debt / org boundary
  • Measurement gap (nobody watched the metric)

Deliverable: Root Cause Register – linking symptoms to process failures.

Phase 4

Remediation – Prioritised Fix Architecture

We score every faultline with RICE‑F (Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort, Forensic Severity multiplier) and sequence them into a three‑horizon roadmap.

Horizon 1 (0–30 days)

Immediate Relief: high‑confidence quick wins with exact implementation specs and success metrics. Labels, errors, missing feedback, accessibility fixes.

Horizon 2 (30–90 days)

Structural Repair: IA restructure, onboarding redesign, trust signals, cross‑platform coherence. Design briefs with before/after hypotheses.

Horizon 3 (90+ days)

Strategic Reconstruction: core mental model realignment, design system overhaul, value proposition clarification. Executive briefs with ROI projections and risk of inaction.

Remediation Cards: Every fix is delivered as a structured card (Faultline ID, evidence, root cause, business impact, annotated recommendation, success metric).

Executive Summary: 10-page C‑suite document with verdict, fault map, top 5 critical faultlines, total cost of inaction, and measurement dashboard.
